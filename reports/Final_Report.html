4. System Architecture & ETL Pipeline

The project follows a classic five-layer data engineering architecture mirroring real-world fintech pipelines at companies like Zerodha, Groww, and Paytm Money.

LAYER 1: EXTRACT
mfapi.in REST API · 10 provided CSV datasets · AMFI India public data · NSE/BSE benchmark CSV
LAYER 2: TRANSFORM
Pandas cleaning · Date parsing · Forward-fill for NAV holidays · Duplicate removal · Type validation · AMFI code cross-validation
LAYER 3: LOAD
SQLite database via SQLAlchemy · 11-table star schema · 89,359 total rows · Indexed on amfi_code + date
LAYER 4: ANALYSE
Jupyter Notebooks · EDA (17 charts) · CAGR/Sharpe/Sortino/Alpha/Beta · VaR/CVaR · Cohort Analysis · Fund Scorecard
LAYER 5: VISUALISE
Streamlit 4-page dashboard · 20+ PNG charts · Interactive slicers · KPI cards · Fund selector · Benchmark comparison

10. Key Findings & Business Insights

1. SIP India Growth Story
Monthly SIP inflows grew 169% in 4 years — from ₹11,517 Cr (Jan 2022) to ₹31,002 Cr (Dec 2025). This demonstrates the deepening of India's equity culture and the success of the SIP habit.
2. AUM Concentration Risk
Top 3 AMCs (SBI, ICICI, HDFC) control ~42% of tracked AUM, creating systemic concentration risk. However, smaller AMCs like Mirae Asset showed the highest percentage growth (2.4x).
3. Return-Risk Trade-off
Small Cap funds delivered 20–23% 3-year CAGR but also had the worst VaR (-2.7%) and max drawdowns (-52%). Investors must explicitly accept this risk profile.
4. Direct Plan Advantage
14 of 40 schemes have expense ratios below 1% — exclusively Direct plans. The average expense ratio difference between Regular and Direct plans is ~0.7–0.9%, compounding significantly over 5+ years.
5. B30 Market Opportunity
B30 cities contribute 34.1% of investment value and are growing faster than T30 metros. This represents a significant underpenetrated market for AMC distribution strategies.

11. Technical Limitations

SIP Continuity Analysis: The synthetic transaction data generates SIPs at roughly monthly intervals. Due to calendar variation (28/30/31-day months, weekends), average gaps naturally exceed 35 days for most investors, inflating the "at-risk" percentage beyond realistic levels. In real AMFI data this would typically be 15–25%.
Alpha/Beta R² Values: The nav_history and benchmark_indices datasets were generated independently, resulting in near-zero correlation (R² ≈ 0). In real-world data, large cap funds show R² of 0.85–0.95 vs Nifty 50. The computed alpha/beta values reflect the synthetic data limitation, not the methodology.
NAV Data Scope: The project tracks 40 of India's 1,908+ registered schemes. Industry-level statistics (₹81 lakh crore AUM, 26.12 crore folios) are sourced from real AMFI data embedded in the datasets, not computed bottom-up from the 40 schemes alone.

12. Conclusion & Recommendations

This project successfully delivered all 7 required deliverables plus the B2 bonus (Streamlit dashboard). The platform demonstrates a production-grade data engineering workflow applicable to real fintech products.

For AMCs and Distributors:

Focus distribution efforts on B30 cities where growth is accelerating. Use the fund recommender framework as a template for personalised advisory tools. Monitor Rolling Sharpe as a leading indicator of fund manager effectiveness rather than point-in-time Sharpe alone.

For Investors:

Direct plans consistently outperform on cost. The composite scorecard provides a balanced view beyond pure return chasing. Risk appetite must be explicitly matched — Small Cap funds with VaR > -2.5% daily require a genuine 5+ year horizon.

Technical Next Steps:

Deploy ETL as a scheduled cron job (B1 bonus). Add Monte Carlo simulation for NAV projections (B3). Implement Markowitz Efficient Frontier for portfolio optimisation (B4). Extend investor transaction data to include real folios via AMFI API.
